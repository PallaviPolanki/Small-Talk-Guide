Small Talk Practice

Build confidence in everyday conversations

How was your day?
Did you have a busy day?
Positive (detailed):
It was great, thanks! I had several meetings, but they went really well. I finished an important project.
Positive (short):
Good, thanks! Pretty busy, but productive.
Neutral:
It was okay. Not too busy, just a normal day.
Busy:
Very busy! I had back-to-back meetings all morning and lots of emails to reply to.
Do you work from home?
How many days a week do you work from home?
Yes (with frequency):
Yes, I work from home three days a week. I come to the office on Tuesdays and Thursdays.
Yes (fully remote):
Yes, I work from home full-time. My company is fully remote.
No:
No, I go to the office every day. I prefer working there because I like the social interaction.
Sometimes:
Sometimes. It depends on my schedule. Usually about two days a week.
What do you usually do on weekdays?
Do you have a daily routine?
Work routine:
I usually wake up at seven, go to work, and get home around six. Then I relax and watch TV.
Varied schedule:
It depends on the day. Some days I work from home, other days I go to the office. I try to exercise in the evenings.
Student life:
I go to classes in the morning and study in the afternoon. Sometimes I meet friends for coffee.
Flexible:
I don't have a strict routine. I like to keep things flexible and spontaneous.
What are you doing at the weekend?
Are you going away this weekend?
Plans (specific):
I'm going to visit my parents. We're planning to have lunch together on Sunday.
Plans (relaxing):
Nothing special. I'm just going to relax at home and catch up on some reading.
Trip:
Yes! I'm going to the mountains for a short break. I'm really looking forward to it.
No plans:
I'm not sure yet. Maybe I'll meet some friends or do some shopping.
Are you doing anything special this weekend?
Do you have any plans?
Special event:
Yes! I'm going to a friend's birthday party on Saturday. It's going to be fun!
Activity planned:
I'm thinking about going to the cinema. There's a new film I want to see.
Nothing special:
Not really. Just the usual stuff – maybe some shopping and cleaning.
Open to ideas:
I haven't decided yet. I'll see how I feel. Maybe I'll do something spontaneous!
What do you like to do in your free time?
Do you have any hobbies?
Active hobbies:
I really enjoy playing tennis and going to the gym. I try to stay active.
Creative hobbies:
I love photography. I take my camera everywhere and enjoy capturing moments.
Relaxing activities:
I like reading and watching films. It helps me relax after work.
Multiple interests:
I have a few hobbies. I play guitar, cook, and I'm learning a new language.
Did you do anything interesting last weekend?
How did you spend your weekend?
Yes (detailed):
Yes! I went hiking with some friends. The weather was beautiful and we had a great time.
Yes (short):
I tried a new restaurant in town. The food was amazing!
Relaxing weekend:
Not really. I just stayed home and relaxed. I needed the rest.
Mixed activities:
I did some gardening on Saturday and watched a film on Sunday. It was nice and peaceful.
Are you planning to start any new hobbies?
What would you like to learn?
Yes (specific):
Yes, I'm thinking about learning to play the piano. I'm going to look for a teacher soon.
Yes (considering):
Maybe. I'd like to try yoga or meditation. I hear it's good for stress.
Not sure:
I'm not sure yet. I'd like to find something creative, but I haven't decided what.
No:
Not at the moment. I'm happy with my current hobbies. I don't have much extra time.
How's the weather today?
Is it nice outside?
Good weather:
It's beautiful! The sun is shining and it's quite warm for this time of year.
Bad weather:
Not great. It's raining and quite cold. I wish it would stop!
Mixed:
It's okay. A bit cloudy, but at least it's not raining.
Typical:
Pretty normal for this season. Cool and a bit windy.
Did you have good weather this weekend?
Was it sunny?
Yes:
Yes, it was lovely! We had sunshine all weekend. Perfect for being outside.
No:
No, unfortunately. It rained both days, so I stayed indoors.
Mixed:
Saturday was nice, but Sunday was rainy. We went out on Saturday though.
Didn't notice:
I'm not sure, to be honest. I was busy working from home, so I didn't pay much attention.
What's the weather forecast for this week?
Is it going to rain?
Good forecast:
It's going to be sunny all week! Perfect weather for outdoor activities.
Bad forecast:
Not good, I'm afraid. It's going to rain for the next few days.
Uncertain:
I'm not sure. I haven't checked the forecast yet. I should probably do that!
Changing:
It's going to be mixed. Rain at the beginning of the week, but it will improve by Friday.
What did you do yesterday?
Did you do anything special?
Work day:
I worked all day. I had a lot of meetings and finished some reports.
Special activity:
Yes, actually! I went to a concert in the evening. It was fantastic!
Regular day:
Nothing special. Just a normal day. I worked, then went home and relaxed.
Social activity:
I met a friend for coffee after work. We had a good chat.
Are you working on any interesting projects?
What do you do at work?
Yes (enthusiastic):
Yes! I'm working on a new marketing campaign. It's really exciting and creative.
Yes (challenging):
I'm working on a big presentation for our clients. It's challenging but interesting.
Routine work:
Not at the moment. Just doing my regular tasks. Nothing too exciting right now.
Multiple projects:
I'm juggling a few projects at once. It's busy, but I like having variety in my work.
Have you picked up any new hobbies recently?
Have you started doing anything different?
Yes (new hobby):
Yes! I've started learning Spanish. I've been taking online classes for two months now.
Yes (rediscovered):
I've started painting again. I used to do it years ago, and I've really enjoyed getting back into it.
Thinking about it:
Not yet, but I've been thinking about joining a sports club. I want to try something new.
No:
No, I haven't had time recently. I've been quite busy with work and other things.
Are you reading anything interesting?
What kind of books do you enjoy?
Yes (current book):
Yes! I'm reading a mystery novel at the moment. It's really gripping – I can't put it down!
Yes (non-fiction):
I'm reading a book about history. It's fascinating and I'm learning a lot.
Not at the moment:
Not right now. I haven't had much time for reading lately. I usually enjoy thrillers though.
Prefer other media:
I don't read much, to be honest. I prefer listening to audiobooks or podcasts.
Have you seen any good films lately?
Have you watched anything you'd recommend?
Yes (enthusiastic):
Yes! I've just seen a brilliant comedy. It was so funny – I laughed the whole way through!
Yes (recommend):
I've watched a really interesting documentary about nature. I'd definitely recommend it.
Not impressed:
I've seen a few films, but nothing special. They've all been quite disappointing, actually.
Too busy:
I haven't had a chance to watch anything recently. I've been too busy with other things.
What are you planning to do next month?
Do you have any trips planned?
Travel plans:
Yes! I'm going to take a week off and travel to Spain. I can't wait!
Special event:
I'm going to attend a friend's wedding. It's going to be a big celebration.
Work focus:
Nothing special planned. I'll be working on a big deadline, so it will be quite busy.
No concrete plans:
I haven't made any plans yet. Maybe I'll organise something with friends.
Did you see the news today?
Did you read anything interesting in the news?
Yes (interested):
Yes, I read about the new technology announcement. It was really interesting!
Yes (local news):
Yes, I saw something about the new shopping centre opening in town. It looks impressive.
No time:
No, I didn't have time this morning. I was too busy getting ready for work.
Not yet:
Not yet. I usually check the news during my lunch break.
Do you follow the news regularly?
How do you stay informed?
Yes (regular):
Yes, I read the news every morning with my coffee. I like to stay informed.
Yes (specific sources):
I follow a few news websites and listen to podcasts during my commute.
Sometimes:
Not every day, but I try to keep up with major stories. I check the headlines online.
No:
Not really. I find the news too negative. I prefer to focus on other things.
Are you going to watch the big event this weekend?
Will you follow the election results?
Yes (excited):
Yes, definitely! I'm going to watch it with friends. It's going to be exciting!
Yes (interested):
Yes, I'm planning to follow the updates. I'm curious about the outcome.
Maybe:
I might check the highlights. I'm not sure if I'll watch the whole thing.
No:
Probably not. I'm not very interested in it. I'll just read about it later.
What kind of music do you like?
Do you have a favourite band or artist?
Specific genre:
I love jazz! I listen to it when I'm working. It helps me concentrate.
Multiple genres:
I like all kinds of music, but I especially enjoy rock and pop.
With favourite artist:
I'm a big fan of classical music. My favourite composer is Beethoven.
Mood-based:
It depends on my mood. Sometimes I like upbeat pop, sometimes quiet acoustic music.
What music did you listen to when you were younger?
Has your taste in music changed?
Changed completely:
I listened to a lot of rock music when I was a teenager. Yes, my taste has changed quite a bit. Now I prefer jazz and classical.
Still similar:
I loved pop music as a teenager. Not really. I still enjoy pop, but I've discovered other genres too.
More varied now:
I mainly listened to hip-hop when I was young. Yes, definitely. I've become more open-minded and now I listen to all kinds of music.
Same preferences:
I listened to classical music even as a child. No, not really. I've always loved classical music and I still do.
Would you like to learn a musical instrument?
Is there an instrument you'd like to play?
Yes (specific):
Yes! I'd love to learn the guitar. I'm going to look for lessons soon.
Yes (dream):
I've always wanted to play the piano. Maybe one day I'll take it up!
Already learning:
Actually, I'm already learning the violin. I started a few months ago and I'm really enjoying it.
No:
Not really. I don't think I have the patience for it. I prefer just listening to music.
Do you like travelling?
What's your favourite place you've visited?
Yes (enthusiastic):
I love travelling! My favourite place is Italy. The food, culture, and scenery are amazing.
Yes (specific):
Yes, I enjoy discovering new places. I especially love visiting historical cities.
Sometimes:
It's okay. I like travelling, but I also enjoy staying at home. My favourite trip was to Japan.
Not much:
Not really. I prefer staying in my own city. I find travelling quite stressful.
Where did you go on your last holiday?
Did you have a good time?
International trip:
I went to Portugal last summer. It was wonderful! The beaches were beautiful and the people were very friendly.
Domestic trip:
I visited the mountains for a week. We went hiking every day. It was very relaxing.
City break:
I spent a weekend in Paris. I visited museums and tried lots of French food. It was amazing!
Stayed home:
I didn't go anywhere. I stayed at home and relaxed. Sometimes that's the best kind of holiday!
Are you planning any trips?
Where would you like to go next?
Specific plans:
Yes! I'm going to Greece in August. I'm going to visit Athens and some islands.
Considering:
I'm thinking about going to Scotland. I'm going to research it this weekend.
Dream destination:
Not yet, but I'd love to visit New Zealand one day. It looks absolutely stunning!
No plans:
Not at the moment. I'm too busy with work. Maybe later this year.
What kind of films do you enjoy?
Do you prefer watching at home or at the cinema?
Specific genre:
I love science fiction films. I enjoy films that make me think about the future.
Multiple genres:
I like comedies and action films. They're entertaining and help me relax.
With preference:
I enjoy dramas the most. I prefer watching at home where it's quiet and comfortable.
Cinema preference:
I like all kinds of films, but I prefer going to the cinema. The big screen makes it more exciting!
What was the last film you watched?
Did you enjoy it?
Loved it:
I watched a thriller last weekend. It was amazing! The plot was really clever and kept me guessing.
Enjoyed it:
I saw a romantic comedy last night. It was fun and made me laugh. Perfect for relaxing!
Disappointed:
I watched an action film yesterday, but it wasn't very good. The story was weak.
Can't remember:
I haven't watched a film in a while, actually. I've been too busy lately.
Are you planning to see any new films?
Is there anything you're excited about?
Yes (specific):
Yes! There's a new superhero film coming out next month. I'm going to see it on opening night!
Maybe:
I'm thinking about it. I heard there's a good documentary coming to the cinema. I might check it out.
At home:
Not at the cinema, but I'm going to watch some films at home this weekend. I have a long list!
No plans:
Not really. I'll probably just watch whatever's on streaming services. I don't plan too far ahead.

Daily Routine & Work

back-to-back meetings - meetings one after another with no break
productive - achieving a lot; getting things done
to catch up on - to do something you haven't had time to do
work from home - working from your house instead of the office
full-time - working the complete work week
social interaction - talking and spending time with other people
spontaneous - done without planning; impulsive
flexible - able to change easily; not fixed

Hobbies & Interests

free time - time when you're not working
to stay active - to exercise regularly
to capture moments - to take photos of special times
to relax - to rest and reduce stress
to look forward to - to feel excited about something in the future
peaceful - quiet and calm
to pick up (a hobby) - to start a new hobby or skill

Weather

forecast - a prediction of future weather
the sun is shining - it's sunny
cloudy - with clouds in the sky
windy - with strong wind
for this time of year - compared to what's normal for this season
to improve - to get better

Recent Activities

to have a good chat - to have a nice conversation
to juggle - to manage multiple things at the same time
variety - different types of things
deadline - the time by which something must be finished
to attend - to go to (an event)
can't wait - very excited about something
gripping - very exciting and holding your attention
can't put it down - so interesting you don't want to stop

Current Events & News

to stay informed - to keep up with what's happening
headlines - the titles of news stories
major stories - important news
to keep up with - to stay updated about
commute - the journey to and from work
outcome - the result; what happens

Music

genre - a style or type of music (rock, jazz, pop)
concert - a live music performance
atmosphere - the mood or feeling of a place
upbeat - lively and energetic
acoustic - music played without electric instruments
to concentrate - to focus attention on something
to take up - to start doing something (a hobby, sport)
open-minded - willing to consider new ideas

Travel

scenery - the natural features of a landscape
historical - relating to history; from the past
city break - a short holiday in a city
stunning - extremely beautiful or impressive
to discover - to find or learn about something new
domestic - within your own country

Films & Cinema

plot - the story of a film or book
thriller - an exciting film with suspense
kept me guessing - made it hard to predict what happens
documentary - a film about real events or people
streaming service - online platform for watching films/shows
opening night - the first night a film is shown

Time Expressions

PAST: yesterday, last weekend, last week, recently, a few days ago
PRESENT: today, right now, at the moment, these days, currently, nowadays
FUTURE: tomorrow, next weekend, next week, soon, in a few days, later
PRESENT PERFECT: just, already, yet, recently, lately, so far, ever, never

Frequency Adverbs

always (100%) - every time
usually (80-90%) - most of the time
often (60-70%) - many times
sometimes (40-50%) - not always, not never
rarely (10%) - not very often
never (0%) - not at any time

Connecting Words & Phrases

but - shows contrast
so - shows result
because - gives a reason
although - shows contrast
actually - introduces surprising info
unfortunately - introduces bad news
luckily - introduces good news
by the way - introduces a new topic

Opinion & Reaction Phrases

I think / I believe - expressing opinion
In my opinion - formal opinion
That's interesting! - positive reaction
Really? - showing surprise
That sounds great! - enthusiastic response
I'm sorry to hear that - showing sympathy

Conversation Extenders

Tell me more about that - asks for details
What happened next? - continues the story
How did that go? - asks about the result
What did you think? - asks for opinion
Why is that? - asks for explanation

Softening & Polite Phrases

I'm not sure, but... - shows uncertainty
maybe / perhaps - suggests possibility
quite / pretty - moderate adjectives
a bit / a little - reduces intensity
to be honest - introduces honest opinion
