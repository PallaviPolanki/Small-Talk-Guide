Business English Small Talk Practice

Build confidence in everyday conversations

Present Tense (Green)
Future Tense (Blue)
How was your day?
Did you have a busy day?
Positive (detailed):
It was great, thanks! I had several meetings, but they went really well. I finished an important project.
Positive (short):
Good, thanks! Pretty busy, but productive.
Neutral:
It was okay. Not too busy, just a normal day.
Busy:
Very busy! I had back-to-back meetings all morning and lots of emails to reply to.
Do you work from home?
How many days a week do you work from home?
Yes (with frequency):
Yes, I work from home three days a week. I come to the office on Tuesdays and Thursdays.
Yes (fully remote):
Yes, I work from home full-time. My company is fully remote.
No:
No, I go to the office every day. I prefer working there because I like the social interaction.
Sometimes:
Sometimes. It depends on my schedule. Usually about two days a week.
What are you doing at the weekend?
Are you going away this weekend?
Plans (specific):
I'm going to visit my parents. We're planning to have lunch together on Sunday.
Plans (relaxing):
Nothing special. I'm just going to relax at home and catch up on some reading.
Trip:
Yes! I'm going to the mountains for a short break. I'm really looking forward to it.
No plans:
I'm not sure yet. Maybe I'll meet some friends or do some shopping.
What do you like to do in your free time?
Do you have any hobbies?
Active hobbies:
I really enjoy playing tennis and going to the gym. I try to stay active.
Creative hobbies:
I love photography. I take my camera everywhere and enjoy capturing moments.
Relaxing activities:
I like reading and watching movies. It helps me relax after work.
Multiple interests:
I have a few hobbies. I play guitar, cook, and I'm learning a new language.
Did you do anything interesting last weekend?
How did you spend your weekend?
Yes (detailed):
Yes! I went hiking with some friends. The weather was beautiful and we had a great time.
Yes (short):
I tried a new restaurant downtown. The food was amazing!
Relaxing weekend:
Not really. I just stayed home and relaxed. I needed the rest.
Mixed activities:
I did some gardening on Saturday and watched a movie on Sunday. It was nice and peaceful.
Are you planning to start any new hobbies?
What would you like to learn?
Yes (specific):
Yes, I'm thinking about learning to play the piano. I'm going to look for a teacher soon.
Yes (considering):
Maybe. I'd like to try yoga or meditation. I hear it's good for stress.
Not sure:
I'm not sure yet. I'd like to find something creative, but I haven't decided what.
No:
Not at the moment. I'm happy with my current hobbies. I don't have much extra time.
How's the weather today?
Is it nice outside?
Good weather:
It's beautiful! The sun is shining and it's quite warm for this time of year.
Bad weather:
Not great. It's raining and quite cold. I wish it would stop!
Mixed:
It's okay. A bit cloudy, but at least it's not raining.
Typical:
Pretty normal for this season. Cool and a bit windy.
Did you have good weather this weekend?
Was it sunny?
Yes:
Yes, it was lovely! We had sunshine all weekend. Perfect for being outside.
No:
No, unfortunately. It rained both days, so I stayed indoors.
Mixed:
Saturday was nice, but Sunday was rainy. We went out on Saturday though.
Didn't notice:
I'm not sure, to be honest. I was busy working from home, so I didn't pay much attention.
What's the weather forecast for this week?
Is it going to rain?
Good forecast:
It's going to be sunny all week! Perfect weather for outdoor activities.
Bad forecast:
Not good, I'm afraid. It's going to rain for the next few days.
Uncertain:
I'm not sure. I haven't checked the forecast yet. I should probably do that!
Changing:
It's going to be mixed. Rain at the beginning of the week, but it will improve by Friday.
Do you have any children?
How old are they?
Yes (with details):
Yes, I have two children. My daughter is seven and my son is four.
Yes (grown up):
Yes, I have one son. He's twenty-five now and lives in another city.
No:
No, I don't have any children yet. Maybe in the future!
No (with pets):
No children, but I have a dog. He's like my child!
Did you see your family recently?
When did you last visit them?
Yes (recent):
Yes, I saw them last weekend. We had a nice dinner together.
Yes (special occasion):
Yes, we all got together for my mother's birthday last month. It was wonderful.
No (long time):
No, not recently. I haven't seen them for a few months. They live quite far away.
Regular contact:
Yes, I saw them yesterday. My parents live nearby, so I visit them often.
Are you planning to visit your family soon?
Will you see them for the holidays?
Yes (specific plans):
Yes, I'm going to visit them next weekend. I'm really looking forward to it.
Yes (holiday plans):
Yes, we're all going to get together for Christmas. It's going to be nice.
Not sure:
I'm not sure yet. It depends on my work schedule. I hope I can visit soon.
No (they're visiting):
Actually, they're going to visit me next month! I'm preparing everything.
What did you do yesterday?
Did you do anything special?
Work day:
I worked all day. I had a lot of meetings and finished some reports.
Special activity:
Yes, actually! I went to a concert in the evening. It was fantastic!
Regular day:
Nothing special. Just a normal day. I worked, then went home and relaxed.
Social activity:
I met a friend for coffee after work. We had a good chat.
Are you working on any interesting projects?
What do you do at work?
Yes (enthusiastic):
Yes! I'm working on a new marketing campaign. It's really exciting and creative.
Yes (challenging):
I'm working on a big presentation for our clients. It's challenging but interesting.
Routine work:
Not at the moment. Just doing my regular tasks. Nothing too exciting right now.
Multiple projects:
I'm juggling a few projects at once. It's busy, but I like having variety in my work.
What are you planning to do next month?
Do you have any trips planned?
Travel plans:
Yes! I'm going to take a week off and travel to Spain. I can't wait!
Special event:
I'm going to attend a friend's wedding. It's going to be a big celebration.
Work focus:
Nothing special planned. I'll be working on a big deadline, so it will be quite busy.
No concrete plans:
I haven't made any plans yet. Maybe I'll organize something with friends.
Did you hear about the news today?
Did you read anything interesting in the news?
Yes (interested):
Yes, I read about the new technology announcement. It was really interesting!
Yes (local news):
Yes, I saw something about the new shopping center opening downtown. It looks impressive.
No time:
No, I didn't have time this morning. I was too busy getting ready for work.
Not yet:
Not yet. I usually check the news during my lunch break.
Do you follow the news regularly?
How do you stay informed?
Yes (regular):
Yes, I read the news every morning with my coffee. I like to stay informed.
Yes (specific sources):
I follow a few news websites and listen to podcasts during my commute.
Sometimes:
Not every day, but I try to keep up with major stories. I check the headlines online.
No:
Not really. I find the news too negative. I prefer to focus on other things.
Are you going to watch the big event this weekend?
Will you follow the election results?
Yes (excited):
Yes, definitely! I'm going to watch it with friends. It's going to be exciting!
Yes (interested):
Yes, I'm planning to follow the updates. I'm curious about the outcome.
Maybe:
I might check the highlights. I'm not sure if I'll watch the whole thing.
No:
Probably not. I'm not very interested in it. I'll just read about it later.

Daily Routine & Work

back-to-back meetings - meetings one after another with no break
productive - achieving a lot; getting things done
to catch up on - to do something you haven't had time to do
work from home - working from your house instead of the office
full-time - working the complete work week
social interaction - talking and spending time with other people

Hobbies & Interests

free time - time when you're not working
to stay active - to exercise regularly
to capture moments - to take photos of special times
to relax - to rest and reduce stress
to look forward to - to feel excited about something in the future
peaceful - quiet and calm

Weather

forecast - a prediction of future weather
the sun is shining - it's sunny
cloudy - with clouds in the sky
windy - with strong wind
for this time of year - compared to what's normal for this season
to improve - to get better

Family

grown up - adult; no longer a child
recently - in the near past
to get together - to meet as a group
quite far away - a long distance
nearby - close; not far
it depends on - conditional; based on something else

Recent Activities

to have a good chat - to have a nice conversation
to juggle - to manage multiple things at the same time
variety - different types of things
deadline - the time by which something must be finished
to attend - to go to (an event)
can't wait - very excited about something

Current Events & News

to stay informed - to keep up with what's happening
headlines - the titles of news stories
major stories - important news
to keep up with - to stay updated about
commute - the journey to and from work
outcome - the result; what happens

Time Expressions

PAST: yesterday, last weekend, last week, recently, a few days ago
PRESENT: today, right now, at the moment, these days, currently, nowadays
FUTURE: tomorrow, next weekend, next week, soon, in a few days, later

Frequency Adverbs

always (100%) - every time
usually (80-90%) - most of the time
often (60-70%) - many times
sometimes (40-50%) - not always, not never
rarely (10%) - not very often
never (0%) - not at any time

Connecting Words & Phrases

but - shows contrast
so - shows result
because - gives a reason
although - shows contrast
actually - introduces surprising info
unfortunately - introduces bad news
luckily - introduces good news
by the way - introduces a new topic

Opinion & Reaction Phrases

I think / I believe - expressing opinion
In my opinion - formal opinion
That's interesting! - positive reaction
Really? - showing surprise
That sounds great! - enthusiastic response
I'm sorry to hear that - showing sympathy

Conversation Extenders

Tell me more about that - asks for details
What happened next? - continues the story
How did that go? - asks about the result
What did you think? - asks for opinion
Why is that? - asks for explanation

Softening & Polite Phrases

I'm not sure, but... - shows uncertainty
maybe / perhaps - suggests possibility
quite / pretty - moderate adjectives
a bit / a little - reduces intensity
to be honest - introduces honest opinion
